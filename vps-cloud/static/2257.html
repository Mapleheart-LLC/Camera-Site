Primary and Secondary Producer Responsibilities

Primary Producers

Creators who upload original sexually explicit content to the Platform act as primary producers under 18 U.S.C. § 2257. As a condition of their creator agreement, all primary producers are required to:

  • Verify and document the age and identity of all performers depicted in any sexually explicit content before uploading.
  • Maintain copies of government-issued photo identification establishing each performer's date of birth and legal name.
  • Maintain records for each performer for a minimum of seven (7) years from the date of last production or, if the content remains available on the Platform, for the duration of availability plus seven years.
  • Provide copies of required records to the Platform's Custodian of Records upon request.

Secondary Producer

mochii.live acts as a secondary producer to the extent it reproduces, re-publishes, or otherwise makes available sexually explicit content uploaded by creators. We maintain records provided by primary producers and ensure that all required labeling is present on applicable content in accordance with 28 C.F.R. § 75.8.

Age Verification Procedures

Before any creator is permitted to upload sexually explicit content to the Platform, we require:

  • Submission of a valid government-issued photo identification document (e.g., passport, national identity card, or driver's license) clearly showing the performer's date of birth.
  • Confirmation that the performer is at least 18 years of age at the time of production.
  • Execution of a performer consent form confirming age and identity.

We do not knowingly permit the upload of sexually explicit content depicting any person under the age of 18. Content that violates this policy will be removed immediately upon discovery, and we will report violations to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and applicable law enforcement authorities.

Exemptions

This statement does not apply to content that does not depict actual sexually explicit conduct as defined by 18 U.S.C. § 2256(2)(A). Visual depictions that are exempt include those depicting simulated sexual conduct or content that does not rise to the level of "sexually explicit conduct" under federal law.
