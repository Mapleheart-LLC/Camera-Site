Overview

mochii.live ("Platform") respects intellectual property rights and expects its users and creators to do the same. In accordance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act of 1998 ("DMCA"), 17 U.S.C. § 512, we will respond expeditiously to claims of copyright infringement communicated to our Designated Copyright Agent using the process described below.

1. Reporting Copyright Infringement

If you believe that content on the Platform infringes your copyright, you may submit a DMCA takedown notice. To be effective under the DMCA, your notice must include all of the following:

  1. A physical or electronic signature of the person authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed (or, if multiple works, a representative list).
  3. Identification of the allegedly infringing material and information reasonably sufficient to permit us to locate it on the Platform (e.g., the URL of the specific page).
  4. Your contact information — name, address, telephone number, and email address.
  5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  6. A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in your notice is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or are authorized to act on the copyright owner's behalf.
⚠️ False Claims: Submitting a DMCA notice for content you know is not infringing may expose you to civil liability and damages under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f).

3. Counter-Notification

If you believe that content you posted was removed or disabled as a result of a mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notification to our Designated Copyright Agent. A valid counter-notification must include:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature.
  2. Identification of the material that was removed or disabled and the location where it appeared before removal.
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  4. Your name, address, and telephone number, and a statement that you consent to jurisdiction in the federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, if outside the US, in any judicial district in which we may be found), and that you will accept service of process from the person who submitted the original notice.

Upon receipt of a valid counter-notification, we will forward it to the original complainant and may restore the removed content within 10–14 business days, unless the complainant notifies us that they have filed a court action to restrain you from the infringing activity.

4. Repeat Infringers

In accordance with the DMCA and applicable law, we maintain a policy to terminate, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts of users who are determined to be repeat infringers of third-party copyrights.
